Recycling Information

Recycling helps reduce waste, conserve resources, and protect the environment. Below are some essential guidelines on how to recycle effectively.

What Can Be Recycled?

  • Paper: Newspapers, magazines, cardboard, office paper, and books.
  • Plastic: Bottles, containers, and packaging (make sure to check recycling symbols).
  • Glass: Bottles and jars (cleaned of food residue).
  • Metal: Aluminum cans, tin cans, and foil.
  • Electronics: Old phones, laptops, and devices should be recycled at special e-waste facilities.

What Cannot Be Recycled?

  • Dirty pizza boxes (food residue contaminates paper recycling).
  • Plastic bags (should be taken to special collection points, not curbside).
  • Light bulbs (check for specific disposal instructions).
  • Styrofoam (often not accepted in curbside recycling programs).
  • Hazardous waste (paint, chemicals, batteriesâ€”requires special disposal).

How to Recycle Properly

Ensure items are clean and dry before recycling. Contamination of recyclables can make the entire batch unrecyclable. Here's how to prepare your recyclables:

  • Rinse containers to remove food or liquid residue.
  • Flatten cardboard boxes to save space.
  • Do not mix recyclables with non-recyclable items.
  • Check local guidelines, as recycling programs may vary by location.
